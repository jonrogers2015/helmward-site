There's a comfortable version of "your own AI": a managed cloud that's branded as personal,
where your files and agents live on someone else's servers behind a friendly UI. It's easy,
it's polished, and it's not actually yours. If their pricing, terms, or business changes, your
"home on the internet" changes with it.

Helmward takes the other road: it runs on **your** hardware. Your machines, your models, your
data. That choice gets more important, not less, as agents get more capable.

## Why ownership matters more as agents get better

A chatbot that answers questions is low-stakes. An agent that reads your files, runs code,
touches your accounts, and acts on your behalf is not. The more an agent can *do*, the more it
matters where it runs and who can see what it's doing.

Local-first means:

- **Your data never leaves unless you send it.** Memory, files, logs — they sit on hardware
  you control, not in a multi-tenant cloud you're renting a corner of.
- **No platform risk.** Nobody can rug your setup with a pricing change or a deprecated
  feature. The system is yours to keep.
- **Real privacy, not a privacy policy.** The strongest guarantee isn't a promise not to look
  — it's that the data was never on their machine to begin with.

## "But the cloud is easier"

It is — and that's the tradeoff most products make for you without asking. Helmward's bet is
that the gap is closing: home hardware is cheap, local models are good enough for the bulk of
the work, and the orchestration can make a few boxes feel like one system. You keep the
convenience of a clean cockpit without handing over the keys.

The brain can still phone the cloud when you want frontier reasoning. The difference is that's
a *choice per task*, not the default for everything you own.

## The honest caveat

Local-first asks a little more of you up front — hardware that stays on, a bit of setup. We
think that's a fair trade for actually owning the thing. And we're building the on-ramps so
it's setup-once, not babysit-forever: workers that relaunch themselves, a runbook for bringing
the whole system up, and sensible defaults so it mostly just runs.

Your agents are going to know a lot about you and do a lot for you. They should live somewhere
you control.
